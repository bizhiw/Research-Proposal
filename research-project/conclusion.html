Conclusion

Doing additional research by conducting interviews would be able to account for the losses by testing the right questions. By triangulating data through further interview research, the structured probing can supplement the interpretive and subjective nature of the case studies. Historical testimony provides a more extensive collection of past data to utilize, but current testimony with structured interviews may provide current and more relevant claim proofs. Case studies, however, provide an initial starting point for understanding the emotional influences of cult members. There is the possibility that future researchers will be inspired to pursue a similar study using the evidence collected from this project
